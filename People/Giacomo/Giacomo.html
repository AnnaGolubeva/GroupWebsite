Giacomo Torlai

About Giacomo

Education

  • Ph.D., University of Waterloo (in Progress)
  • M.Sc., Ludwig Maximilian University Munich (2014)
  • B.Sc., University of Florence (2011)

    • Research
    My primary research interest are quantum systems comprising many degrees of freedom, ranging from condensed matter physics to quantum technologies. I am especially interested in the development of numerical techniques to uncover emergent phenomena in strongly-correlated systems, such as topological order, and to probe and characterize near-term quantum hardware. At the University of Waterloo, we have been designing algorithms and performing numerical experiments using a combination of Monte Carlo methods and modern Machine Learning techniques. In particular, we developed a framework based on stochastic neural networks for quantum error correction of stabilizer codes and quantum state tomography of entangled many-body systems.
